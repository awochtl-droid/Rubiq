01 Brand Overview

Rubiq Financial Group is a private wealth advisory firm offering investment advisory, real estate investment, and credit strategies to individuals and institutions. The brand identity projects precision, authority, and approachability — a modern financial firm that combines institutional discipline with personal service.

The Rubiq brand communicates: trust, sophistication, and strategic intelligence. Every visual and written touchpoint should reinforce these values.

Brand Positioning

Rubiq Financial Group occupies the space between institutional asset management and boutique private wealth advisory. The brand is forward-thinking and data-driven, yet personal and client-focused. It avoids both the stiff formality of large wirehouse firms and the casual tone of fintech startups.

02 Logo

Primary Logo — Landscape (Preferred)

The landscape configuration is the primary logo and should be used in most contexts, including website headers, email signatures, presentations, and digital marketing.

Secondary Logo — Stacked / Vertical

The stacked logo is used when horizontal space is constrained, such as square social profile images, mobile app icons, or embossed materials.

White Logo Variants — For Dark Backgrounds

On dark backgrounds (#0F1724, #1C2D44, or dark gradients), use the white logo variant to maintain legibility and contrast.

Logo Construction

The Rubiq mark consists of two elements: (1) the geometric icon — a stylized 'R' set inside nested square brackets rendered in Royal Blue, with gold/bronze accent elements on the lower portions — and (2) the wordmark 'RUBIQ' in bold spaced capitals followed by 'Financial Group' in a lighter weight beneath.

The icon and wordmark must always appear together. Never use the 'R' icon alone as the primary identifier in client-facing materials.

Clear Space

Always maintain clear space around the logo equal to the height of the capital 'R' in the wordmark on all four sides. No other graphic elements, text, or imagery should intrude within this zone.

Logo Don'ts

  • Do not stretch or distort the logo proportions
  • Do not rotate the logo
  • Do not apply drop shadows, outlines, or special effects
  • Do not change the logo colors — use only approved color variants
  • Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds
  • Do not recreate the logo in other typefaces or software
  • Do not use the icon mark alone to represent the company in client-facing materials

Color Usage Ratios

Web: Dark ink backgrounds dominate hero and alternating sections (~40%). Parchment/white for light sections (~30%). Royal Blue for headings, CTAs, and accents (~20%). Warm Gold as accent for dividers, labels, and highlights (~10%).

Print: Royal Blue should dominate (60%). Warm Gold is an accent (20%). White provides breathing room (15%). Grays handle supporting text (5%).

Never use the accent gold as a background for body text.

04 Typography

The typography system pairs a refined serif display face with a clean sans-serif body font. This dual-font approach creates clear visual hierarchy — the serif signals authority and craft, while the sans-serif ensures readability across all screen sizes.

Display Typeface — Playfair Display (Web)

Playfair Display is the display typeface for all website headings, section titles, and pull quotes. Its high-contrast strokes and elegant serifs project the sophistication and authority central to the Rubiq brand.

Clarity for Complex Capital

Playfair Display — 400, 500, 600, 700 weights + italics

@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Playfair+Display:ital,wght@0,400;0,500;0,600;0,700;1,400;1,500;1,600&display=swap');
font-family: 'Playfair Display', serif;

Body Typeface — Inter (Web)

Inter is the body typeface for all web and digital interfaces. It renders excellently at all screen sizes and provides the clean, professional counterpoint to Playfair Display's ornamental character.

The quick brown fox jumps over the lazy dog. 0123456789

Inter — 300 (Light), 400 (Regular), 500 (Medium), 600 (SemiBold) weights

@import url('https://fonts.googleapis.com/css2?family=Inter:wght@300;400;500;600&display=swap');
font-family: 'Inter', -apple-system, BlinkMacSystemFont, sans-serif;

Print Typeface — Calibri

Calibri is the primary typeface for all documents, presentations, and print materials. It is universally available across Microsoft Office, web, and Adobe environments and maintains brand consistency.

05 Graphic Elements & Layout

The Rubiq visual system uses a distinctive ghosted bracket motif — derived from the 'R' icon — as a background watermark element. This appears in the lower-right corner of stationery, envelopes, and presentation slides to add depth without distraction.

Background Bracket Motif

The nested bracket watermark should appear at 8–12% opacity in Royal Blue. It should never compete with content. Do not recolor it gold or use it at full opacity. On dark backgrounds, use at 15% white opacity.

The ghosted bracket graphic is a subtle cue — it reinforces brand identity without dominating the page. Use it consistently on formal documents and presentation templates.

Section Divider / Rule

The decorative section rule is a signature element used on the website to separate content areas. It uses the blue-to-gold gradient and should be consistent across all pages.

08 Voice & Tone

The Rubiq voice reflects the dual identity of the firm: rigorous analytical capability paired with approachable, client-first communication. Written materials should feel authoritative but never stiff, and professional but never cold.

Core Voice Principles

Confident, not arrogant

State positions clearly and support them with reasoning. Avoid hedging excessively, but acknowledge complexity where it exists.

Precise, not dense

Use accurate financial language appropriate to the audience. Translate jargon for general audiences without being condescending.

Warm, not casual

Maintain professionalism while being genuinely personable. Client letters should feel personal and considered, not templated.

Forward-looking, not speculative

Lead with opportunity and strategy. Avoid alarmist language or uncalibrated predictions.

Writing for Multiple Audiences

Rubiq serves clients across multiple sophistication levels. The same market insight or trade recommendation should be adaptable into at least three registers:

RegisterDescription
Formal / TechnicalFull analytical context, data citations, risk disclosures, and precise financial terminology. For investment committee level or institutional audiences.
Condensed ProfessionalCore thesis and key supporting points. Assumes financial literacy. Suitable for most advisory clients and RIA partners.
Plain LanguageClear, direct explanation without jargon. Focuses on what it means for the client's portfolio. For general audience or introductory communications.
